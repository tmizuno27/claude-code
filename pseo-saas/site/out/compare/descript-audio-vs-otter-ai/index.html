Where Descript pulls ahead is with exclusive access to Text To Speech and Voice Cloning and Noise Removal and Podcast Editing and Custom Voices, which can be a deciding factor for teams that rely on these capabilities. On the other hand, Otter.ai uniquely offers Api Access and Real Time, giving it an edge for users who prioritize these areas. Who Should Choose Which?

The ideal user for each platform differs considerably. Descript is best suited for podcast editors, audio content creators, making it a strong choice if you fall into any of these categories. Otter.ai, meanwhile, shines for meeting transcription, journalists and students, which means it may be the better pick if your needs align with those use cases. Founded in 2017, Descript describes itself as "Edit audio by editing text with AI." Otter.ai, established in 2016, positions itself as "AI meeting assistant that records and transcribes." Both platforms have been in the market for a similar duration, giving each ample time to refine their offerings and build a loyal user base.

Our Verdict

After analyzing all the data, **Descript** comes out slightly ahead in this comparison, thanks to more features (6 vs 3), availability of a free plan. However, this does not mean Otter.ai is a poor choice — far from it. Otter.ai excels in its own right, particularly for meeting transcription and journalists and students. Our recommendation: if you value text-based audio editing is revolutionary, go with Descript. If excellent real-time transcription matters more to you, Otter.ai is the way to go. Either way, both are solid platforms that have earned their place in the market.
