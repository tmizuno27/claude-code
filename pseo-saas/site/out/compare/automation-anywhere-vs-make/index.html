Who Should Choose Which?

The ideal user for each platform differs considerably. Automation Anywhere is best suited for large enterprises, IT operations, shared services, making it a strong choice if you fall into any of these categories. Make (Integromat), meanwhile, shines for power users, agencies, complex automations, which means it may be the better pick if your needs align with those use cases. Founded in 2003, Automation Anywhere describes itself as "Enterprise RPA platform combining bots, AI, and process intelligence." Make (Integromat), established in 2012, positions itself as "Visual platform for designing, building, and automating complex workflows." With 9 years between them, Automation Anywhere brings the maturity and proven track record of a veteran platform, while Make (Integromat) offers the fresh perspective and modern architecture of a newer entrant.

Our Verdict

After analyzing all the data, **Make (Integromat)** comes out slightly ahead in this comparison, thanks to higher user ratings (9.0 vs 8.0), availability of a free plan. However, this does not mean Automation Anywhere is a poor choice — far from it. Automation Anywhere excels in its own right, particularly for large enterprises and IT operations. Our recommendation: if you value superior visual workflow builder, go with Make (Integromat). If market-leading rpa capabilities matters more to you, Automation Anywhere is the way to go. Either way, both are solid platforms that have earned their place in the market.
