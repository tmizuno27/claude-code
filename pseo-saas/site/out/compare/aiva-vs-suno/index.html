Who Should Choose Which?

The ideal user for each platform differs considerably. AIVA is best suited for film and game composers, orchestral and classical music, making it a strong choice if you fall into any of these categories. Suno, meanwhile, shines for creating music without instruments, content creators needing custom music, which means it may be the better pick if your needs align with those use cases. Founded in 2016, AIVA describes itself as "AI composer for emotional soundtracks and music." Suno, established in 2023, positions itself as "Create full songs with AI from a text prompt." With 7 years between them, AIVA brings the maturity and proven track record of a veteran platform, while Suno offers the fresh perspective and modern architecture of a newer entrant.

Our Verdict

After analyzing all the data, **Suno** comes out slightly ahead in this comparison, thanks to higher user ratings (8.0 vs 7.0), more features (2 vs 1), more competitive pricing, availability of a free plan. However, this does not mean AIVA is a poor choice — far from it. AIVA excels in its own right, particularly for film and game composers and orchestral and classical music. Our recommendation: if you value incredibly easy to use, go with Suno. If excellent orchestral compositions matters more to you, AIVA is the way to go. Either way, both are solid platforms that have earned their place in the market.
