# How I Organize My Side Hustle with Notion (And Why Spreadsheets Weren't Enough)

*For: Medium / Dev.to*

---

I run multiple side hustles alongside other work. At one point, I was managing a digital product business, freelance projects, and a content-based website — all simultaneously.

For the first few months, my "system" was a mix of Google Sheets, Apple Notes, and optimistic memory. It worked until it didn't.

The breaking point came when I forgot to invoice a client for 6 weeks, missed a product launch deadline I'd set for myself, and realized I had no idea which of my three hustles was actually profitable per hour.

That's when I moved everything into Notion. Here's exactly how my system works.

---

## The Problem with Spreadsheets

Don't get me wrong — Google Sheets is powerful. But for managing a side hustle, it has three fatal flaws:

1. **No relational data.** You can't click on a client name in your revenue sheet and see all their projects, invoices, and communication history. Everything lives in flat tables.

2. **No multiple views of the same data.** A spreadsheet is a spreadsheet. You can't toggle between a kanban view for project status, a calendar view for deadlines, and a table view for financial data — all from the same underlying dataset.

3. **No templates for workflows.** Every new project means copying cells, adjusting formulas, and hoping you didn't break something.

Notion solves all three. And the free plan handles everything I need.

## My Notion Setup: The Overview

My workspace has four main sections:

### 1. The Dashboard

One page I see every morning. It shows:
- **Today's tasks** (filtered from my master task database)
- **This week's deadlines** (across all hustles)
- **Revenue this month** (rolled up from all income entries)
- **Unpaid invoices** (with days outstanding)
- **Active projects** (kanban view, filtered to "in progress")

Opening this page takes 2 seconds and tells me everything I need to know. No app switching, no mental assembly required.

### 2. Per-Hustle Tracking

Each side hustle has its own section with:

**Revenue database** — Every payment received, tagged by hustle, with date and amount. A rollup formula shows monthly/quarterly/annual revenue per hustle automatically.

**Expense database** — Every cost, categorized (tools, marketing, materials). Linked to the relevant hustle.

**Time log** — Hours spent per day, tagged by hustle and activity type (creation, admin, marketing). This is the game-changer.

**The formula that matters most:**

```
Effective hourly rate = Total Revenue / Total Hours
```

This one number tells you more about your side hustle than any other metric. When I first calculated it, I discovered one hustle earning $800/month at 50 hours/month ($16/hour) and another earning $400/month at 8 hours/month ($50/hour). The "smaller" hustle was 3x more valuable per hour.

I immediately shifted my time allocation.

### 3. Project Pipeline

Every deliverable — whether it's a product to ship, an article to publish, or a client project to complete — goes into a unified project database with:

- **Status:** Idea → Planning → In Progress → Review → Complete
- **Hustle:** Which business it belongs to
- **Priority:** P1 (this week), P2 (this month), P3 (someday)
- **Progress:** Milestone-based percentage (seeing 70% complete is incredibly motivating)

The kanban view shows me all active projects across all hustles at a glance. The calendar view shows deadlines. The table view lets me sort and filter however I need.

### 4. The Weekly Review

Every Sunday, 20 minutes:

1. **Clear the inbox** — process any loose notes, tasks, or ideas captured during the week
2. **Review completed tasks** — celebrate wins (seriously, this matters)
3. **Check the numbers** — revenue, hours, effective hourly rate per hustle
4. **Set next week's focus** — pick the ONE thing per hustle that moves the needle most
5. **Update project statuses** — make sure everything reflects reality

This ritual is the glue. Without it, the system would slowly drift into inaccuracy.

## The Results After 6 Months

**Before Notion:**
- Frequently forgot tasks and deadlines
- No idea which hustle was most profitable
- Spent ~1 hour/day on "admin" (searching for things, updating spreadsheets, context switching)
- Income felt random and uncontrollable

**After Notion:**
- Zero missed deadlines (the dashboard makes them impossible to miss)
- Clear profitability data per hustle — reallocated time to highest-ROI activities
- Admin time: ~15 minutes/day
- Income grew 40% in 6 months — not from working harder, but from working on the right things

The 40% growth came almost entirely from two changes: (1) spending more time on my highest $/hour hustle, and (2) actually following up on unpaid invoices consistently.

## How to Build This Yourself

If you want to build a similar system from scratch:

1. **Start with ONE database:** Tasks. Everything flows from tasks.
2. **Add a "Hustle" property** to categorize tasks, revenue, and expenses by business.
3. **Create your dashboard page** with filtered views of your databases.
4. **Add the time log** after 2 weeks — once the habit of using Notion is established.
5. **Do weekly reviews** from week 1. This is non-negotiable.

The whole build takes 3-5 hours if you know Notion. Longer if you're learning as you go.
